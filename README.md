BriefSignal: AI Creative Intelligence for Agile Advertising
Market: Advertising agencies and brand marketing teams building paid social campaigns.
The Problem: Automated Execution, Stagnant Intelligence
The agency workflow is automated at the tail end, but broken at the start. While execution and production are automated, the upstream intelligence layer remains painfully manual.
The Velocity Gap: Trends die in 3–5 days; traditional agency brief cycles take 1–2 weeks.
The Generic Output: 97.7% of agencies use AI, yet 84% launch generic campaigns. Tools like Fluency handle execution, but the creative input remains uninspired.
The Workflow Friction: Strategists search outdated press, account managers manually scrape competitive data before client calls, and pitch teams have 72 hours to master a new category.
Subscription Bloat: IT departments juggle five disconnected subscriptions (Brandwatch, Pathmatics), none of which answer the core question: What should the creative actually say?
Where Exa Fits: Owning Layer 1
The agency workflow has five layers: intelligence → brief → production → execution → reporting. Layers 3–5 are automated. Exa solves Layer 1 to transform Layer 2.
A solution built entirely on Exa's core API can power the full creative intelligence workflow. Exa handles retrieval; the application layer handles synthesis:
Instant Brief Cards: Exa searches trade press, brand newsrooms, and open-web signals simultaneously — returning the raw intelligence that gets synthesized into structured brief cards in under 60 sec.
Trend Monitors: Exa continuously monitors high-signal sources and alerts teams the moment a relevant trend breaks, before it peaks.
Deep Analysis: Exa Agents conduct automated deep-dives into competitive landscapes, category shifts, and audience sentiment — research that would otherwise take a strategist half a day.

Market Size: The global advertising intelligence market is $8B, growing at 15%+ annually.
The Beachhead: Creative intelligence has no dominant player — the entry point is AI-native agencies who already build with APIs, the prize is Fortune 500 brand teams who run hundreds of briefs a month and have budgets to match.
Sales Strategy & 30-Day Pilot Blueprint
The buyer is a VP of Strategy, not an engineer. Sales conversations must pivot away from API specs and toward workflow outcomes.
Handling Objections: Counter "we have social listening" by positioning Exa as the open-web complement to Brandwatch's closed-platform tracking. Sell the workflow, not the technical infrastructure.
The Pilot Win Condition: Establish baseline metrics for briefs-per-week and time-per-brief. Run for 30 days. The trial is a success if one campaign outperforms the team's average, traceable to an Exa signal that manual research would have missed.
Secondary Metrics: Track hours saved per person per day, and whether that time translates into one more client won or a measurable lift in Return on Ad Spend (ROAS).
